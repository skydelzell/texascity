History of Texas City


Texas City derives its name from when Texas settlers wanted to wanted to escape from Texas City, Texas due to the extreme heat and harsh life of Texas City, Texas. They traveled by wagons and horse back dealing with harsh weather and troubles in 1800s. Once in Iowa, the settled Texas City! The first winter was extremely hard for the former Texans. One of the first settlers Paul Harrison wrote in his journal, "I had never seen such whiteness. When I went outside everything was just white and the bitterly cold." After that winter, they learned to love the snow and became much better at insulating their structures. Since then, Texas Citizens have taken pride in their ability to withstand all sorts of weather.

Above is the first city hall building in Texas City! It was built in 1852 by the first settlers from Texas City, Texas. It was designed by James Henderson and his wife Elizabeth, often referred to as the parents of Texas City. Named after the chief designer, Henderson Hall has stood proudly in the center of town for many years. Today, you can tour the building and learn more about the rich history of Texas City.
